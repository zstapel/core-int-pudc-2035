Similes are a form of metaphor that explicitly use connecting words (such as like, as, so, than, or carious verbs such as resemble), though these specific words are not always neccassay. While similes are easily used as forms of poetry that compare the intamate and the living, there are also trees in which similes and are used for harmonous perposes and comparison.
