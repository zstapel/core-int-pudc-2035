Figure of Speech n.1

Alliteration

Alliteration is the conspicous repetition of identical initial consonant sounds in successive or closely assoviated syllables within a group of words, even those spelled differently.
As a method of linking words for effect, alliteration is also called head rhyme or initial rhyme. "Alliteration" is from the Latin word littera, "letter of the alphabet"; it was first coined in a Latin dialogue by the Italian humanist Giovanni Pontano in the 15th century.
